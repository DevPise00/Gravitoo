About the Disease
Wheat Leaf Blight (Alternaria triticina) causes water-soaked lesions that turn yellow-brown. It spreads rapidly under humid conditions (>70% RH) and can reduce yield by 30–45% if left untreated. Early intervention is critical.
Treatment Plan
1
Remove and destroy infected leaves to prevent spore spread. Do not compost.
2
Apply Mancozeb 75WP @ 2g/litre water as foliar spray. Repeat every 7 days.
3
Reduce irrigation frequency. Avoid overhead watering during evening hours.
